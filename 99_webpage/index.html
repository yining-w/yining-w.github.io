About slum households and prevalence in India

A slum household lacks at least one of the following conditions:

1. Sufficient Living Area

(40 percent of households in India have more than three people sharing a room)

2. Access to Improved Water and Sanitation

(22 percent don't have access to water that is sufficient, affordable, and can be obtained without extreme effort,
and a private toilet, or a public one shared with a reasonable number of people)

3. Housing Durability

(40 percent don't have a permanent structure providing protection from extreme climatic conditions)

4. Security of Tenure

(Not all households are guaranteed secure tenure status and protection against forced eviction)

The Learning Crisis is Visible

Socioeconomic quartiles are uneven

30 percent of slum households are in the bottom wealth quintile compared to 5 percent of non-slum households.

While 58 percent of non-slum households are among the wealthiest families, compared to just 15 percent of slum households.
