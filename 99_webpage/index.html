About slum households and prevalence in India

A slum household is defined as a group of individuals living under the same roof lacking one or more of the following conditions:

1. Sufficient Living Area

(no more than three people sharing a room)

2. Access to Improved Water and Sanitation

(water that is sufficient, affordable, and can be obtained without extreme effort)

(a private toilet, or a public one shared with a reasonable number of people)

3. Housing Durability

(a permanent structure providing protection from extreme climatic conditions)

4. Security of Tenure

(de facto or de jure secure tenure status and protection against forced eviction)

Slum hh are 6 times more likely to be socially disadvantaged (Q1) compared to non poor urban households. 58 percent of non poor households are in to top quartile compared to 15 percent of poor households.
